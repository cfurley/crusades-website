Levant

The Levant is the region of the Middle East which contains modern day Israel, Jordan, Lebanon, Syria and Palestine. It is famous for containing cities such as Jerusalem and Damascus. It is a major religous region in the world, and very close to the cradle of civilization.

Kosher

Kosher is a dietary ruleset rooted in Abrahamic Religion. Some of the main rules of Kosher is to not mix meat and dairy, and not to eat pork. Kosher is an important part of many Jewish lives.

Crusades

The Crusades where Holy Wars conducted by the Catholic Church to take the Holy Land from the Muslims. The Crsuades led to the creation of the Crusader states and was a time of warfare within the Levant.

Jerusalem

Jerusalem is a major holy city to the Abrahamic religions. For the Crusaders, Jerusalem was so important that it was the main objective of their campaign.

Seljuk Turks

The Seljuk Turks ruled over the Levant prior to the Crusaders. They were Muslim rulers, and shared a border to the Fatimid Muslims in Egypt. Under rulership of the Seljuk Turks, religous freedoms were alloed.

Knights Templar

The Knights Templar were a mliitary order set in place to defend the Holy Land. This is the most famous of the military orders and has many modern pop fiction references.

Knights Hospitaller

A Military Order of Knights formed in the Crusader State of Jerusalem. The task of the Hospitallers was to defend the Holy Land. This order is probably the second most famous behind the Knights Templars.

Mamluk Sultanate

The Mamluk Sultanate ruled Eqypt, Syria, and the Levant between 1250 and 1517. The Mamluks themselves were freed slave soldiers. The Mamluks drove our the Crusaders, and were a very strong Muslim power in the region while they ruled.
